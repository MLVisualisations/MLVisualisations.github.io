K Nearest Neighbours

K nearest neighbours is the simplest classification algorithm. Given a similarity measure (i.e. distance measure), we classify a new element as the mode of its k nearest neighbours in our training set. The easiest similarity measure to visualise is Euclidean distance, i.e., the length of the straight line between two points. Consequently, throughout the remainder of this post, we will use Euclidean distance, but please keep in mind that any similarity measure could be used.

Consider a training set of two dimensional vectors belonging to one of two classes, red or blue. Which class does the green cross belong to?

Possibly the most obvious solution is to look at its nearest neighbour. This requires us to compute the distance between the green cross and all other points in our training set.
