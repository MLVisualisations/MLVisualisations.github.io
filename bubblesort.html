Let's consider an example case for using the bubblesort algorithm.

Consider the following unsorted array: $$ x = [1, 4, 24, 21, 53, 102, 6, 42, 16, 99].$$

To sort $x$ we can use the bubblesort algorithm. This sorting scheme compares adjacent elements for the entire array, eventually leading to a sorted array.

Example:

(1) Compare x[0] = 1 with x[1] = 4. Since 1 < 4, stop and move onto next entry (same applies with x[1] and x[2]).

(2) Compare x[2] = 24 with x[3] = 21. Since 24 > 21, we swap their values, resulting in: $$x = [1, 4, 21, 24, 53, 102, 6, 42, 16, 99].$$ *** FINISH ***
